Simple Guide — What Each Field Means

This guide explains each field in simple English so anyone can use this system. You can get most of these numbers from a hospital test result or a doctor.

Pregnancies

Simple meaning: How many times the woman has been pregnant before.

Example: If a woman has given birth 3 times and had 1 miscarriage, the number is 4. If the patient is a man, or has never been pregnant, put 0.

Where to get it: Ask the patient directly.

Glucose (mg/dL)

Simple meaning: The amount of sugar in the person's blood. This is the most important test for diabetes (sugar disease).

Normal range: Between 70–140 mg/dL. If the number is higher than 140, the person may have too much sugar in their blood.

Where to get it: Blood test at any hospital, clinic, or lab. They call it "fasting blood sugar" or "glucose test."

Blood Pressure (mm Hg)

Simple meaning: How hard the blood is pushing against the walls of the body's blood pipes (vessels). We need only the lower number (the second/bottom number).

Example: If the doctor says your BP is "120/80", enter 80 here. Normal is around 60–80.

Where to get it: Any hospital, pharmacy, or health centre can check BP. It's the machine they wrap around your arm.

Skin Thickness (mm)

Simple meaning: How thick the fat under the skin is at the back of the upper arm. This helps know if the person has too much body fat.

Normal range: Around 10–50 mm. A doctor or nurse pinches the skin on the back of the arm and measures it with a small tool.

Where to get it: A hospital or clinic. If you don't have it, you can enter 20 as an average.

Insulin (μU/ml)

Simple meaning: Insulin is a chemical the body makes to control sugar in the blood. This number shows how much insulin is in the blood 2 hours after eating.

Normal range: Around 16–166 μU/ml. Too little insulin means the body cannot control sugar properly.

Where to get it: Blood test at a hospital or lab. Ask for "insulin level test." If you don't have it, you can enter 80.

BMI (Body Mass Index)

Simple meaning: A number that shows if the person's weight is healthy for their height. It tells you if someone is too thin, normal, overweight, or obese (too fat).

BMI Guide:

  • Below 18.5 → Too thin (Underweight)
  • 18.5 – 24.9 → Normal / Healthy ✅
  • 25.0 – 29.9 → Overweight ⚠️
  • 30.0 and above → Obese (too fat) 🔴

How to calculate: Weight (kg) ÷ (Height in metres × Height in metres).
Example: 70kg, 1.65m tall → 70 ÷ (1.65 × 1.65) = 25.7

Where to get it: Weigh yourself and measure your height, then calculate, or ask a doctor/nurse.

Diabetes Pedigree Function

Simple meaning: This is a number that shows how likely the person is to have diabetes because of family history. If your parents, grandparents, or siblings have diabetes, this number will be higher.

Typical range: Between 0.08 and 2.42.
• Low (0.1–0.3) → Few family members have diabetes
• Medium (0.3–0.7) → Some family members have it
• High (0.7+) → Many close family members have diabetes

Where to get it: This is usually calculated by a doctor or from medical records. If you're unsure, use 0.5 as average.

Age (Years)

Simple meaning: How old the person is in years. Older people are more likely to develop diabetes, especially after age 40–45.

Example: If the person was born in 1990 and this year is 2025, their age is 35.

Where to get it: Ask the patient their age.

Where can I do these tests in Nigeria?

You can get most of these numbers (Glucose, Blood Pressure, Insulin) by visiting any General Hospital, Primary Health Centre, Private Clinic, or Medical Laboratory near you. Ask for a "diabetes screening test" or "blood sugar test." It usually costs between ₦1,000 – ₦5,000.
